Costume Contest

Sponsored by Trinity Treasures (cosplay shop), Ouji Hime (wig shop), Hands Craft Shop and Haralds Fabric Warehouse. Designers of costumes will compete for prizes, and parade their costumes for the discerning SANCon attendees. Entry conditions in the rules. Costumers who are not competitive or have bought costumes may still parade on the stage before the competition, but must still register. Please note this on the entry form.

When: 2pm, Sunday
Where:

  • Pre-judging: KE07, 2pm
  • Contest: KG05, 4pm

Rules:

  1. One entry per designer.
  2. The categories are Cosplay, and Other Costumes.
    1. Cosplays are those costumes of characters from works of fiction. This includes from books, TV, movies, comics, manga, and radio, amongst others.
    2. Other costumes includes all other costumes, including, but not limited to, steampunk, historical and original works. Fashions are not permitted.
  3. Entries in either category are eligible for the open placing and prizes, as well as category prizes.
  4. The majority of the costume should be made or modified by the designer.
  5. The use of a model to model a designers creation is allowed.
  6. Judging:
    1. Cosplays will be judged on the quality workmanship, including difficulty, and the accuracy (compared to supplied references.)
    2. Other costumes will be judged on workmanship and on artistry/interpretation (or accuracy as required by the type of costume. Eg. Historical).
  7. The judges will attempt to judge to the spirit of the rules, but reserve the right to apply their own judgement.
  8. Any contestant that feels unhappy should talk to the organiser.
  9. The winners will be Best open, Runner up open, Best Cosplay, Best Costume, Runner up best cosplay, Runner up best costume.
  10. There are significant prizes. The prizes shall be divided up as fairly as possible, based on the final level of sponsorship available.
  11. All contestants (and models) must attend the pre-judging at 2pm Sunday.
  12. All models will parade at the cosplay competition on the main stage on Sunday. Announcement of winners, and handing out of prizes will take place then.
  13. The entry form must be handed to the registration desk no later than 1:30pm Sunday, or presented to the judges at 2pm sharp in the pre-judging room. A copy of the entry form may be obtained from the registration desk, or may be downloaded from http://sdrv.ms/183TDxF.
  14. References:
    1. Cosplay entries must have reference material attached to the entry. It should be pictures of at least two angles of the character if available. A written description of the character from a book, play or radio show would also be acceptable. Please try to be brief. Also mention if you are doing an interpretation of a character
    2. Costume entries must include a description of the designers inspiration/vision/intent, ie. what they set out to create. Alternatively it may contain reference pictures or description, eg. for historical costumes.
