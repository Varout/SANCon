Workshops

Want to learn something new? Maybe you want to try your hand at making some steampunk-style jewellery, or find out how chain-mail is actually made. SANCon's premium workshop circuit allows you to learn a little, and take home something special for your time.

Panels & more...

Academic presentation: "It's all geek to me"

When: Saturday
Description: Superhero comic book fandom is part of an intersecting matrix of related fandoms and interests which can be considered a resistant subculture (“geekdom”). Geekdom can be considered a resistant subculture because it involves the alternative uses of media texts, group practices, and because such obsessive interests are not seen as ‘normal’. The nature of comic book ‘geekdom,’ is explored by looking at the ways in which comic book readers actively constitute themselves as members of a subculture through their (gendered) interactions with other comic book readers.
Through examining fan interactions I examine the ways that participants ‘do geekdom’ to create a feeling of connection within the context of focus groups to constitute a group geek identity. Geek communities have a considerable gender imbalance, so I discuss how doing geekdom was a gendered practice. At different times they utilised a wide range of (sub)cultural resources to create a feeling of connectedness within the group, while at others those same resources were used to compete, exclude or marginalise individuals within the research groups.
