Cosplay & Costume Contests



The costume competition is a feature of SANCon every year. We are always excited to see what people can create. This is a nice friendly competition, so please enter. The rules can be found below.

There are a number of Workshops, panels, talks and movies of interest to the costumer. A full list can be found on the [events] page.

A list of those confirmed so far:

  • [A basic patterning class] Using and altering paper patterns.
  • [Make up for stage].
  • [Full face prosthetics] How to change your face into something out of the movies.

We are planning lots more classes and presentations, and will update the site when details are confirmed. However if you have some aspect of costuming or props you want teach or talk about then send us details.

Contest Rules:


  1. One entry per designer.
  2. The majority of the costume should be made or modified by the designer.
  3. The use of a model to model a designerâ€™s creation is allowed.
  4. Costumes will be judged on workmanship and on artistry/interpretation.
  5. The judges will attempt to judge to the spirit of the rules, but reserve the right to apply their own judgement.
  6. Any queries or concerns contact the organiser.
  7. There will be First, Second and Third Prizes.
  8. The prizes shall be divided up as fairly as possible, based on the final level of sponsorship available.
  9. All contestants (and models) must attend the judging at the arranged time on Sunday afternoon.
  10. All models will parade at the cosplay parade on the main stage on Sunday. Announcement of winners, and handing out of prizes will take place then.
  11. The entry form must be handed to the registration desk no later than ?pm Sunday, or presented to the judges in the judging room. A copy of the entry form may be obtained from the registration desk, or may be downloaded from here. Please right click the link and save as link, then open in Adobe Reader.
  12. References:
    1. Cosplay/Historical garb entries must have reference material attached to the entry. It should be pictures of at least two angles of the character/original garb if available. A written description of the character from a book, play or radio show would also be acceptable. Please try to be brief. Also mention if you are doing an interpretation of a character.
    2. Other costume entries must include a description of the designers inspiration/vision/intent, ie. what they set out to create.
  13. You agree as a condition of entry that SANCon may take photos of your model in costume. They may use the photos for promoting SANCon, including uploading to and display on the SANCon website, and in printed materials.
